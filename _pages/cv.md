Profile
======
I'm an experienced economist and public policy professional with expertise in
housing, land-use, transport and urban economics. I combine quantitative
analysis and modelling with policy communication, including media and public
speaking for non-technical audiences.

Education
======
* Bachelor of Economics (Honours Class 1 in Econometrics), University of New South Wales, 2018-2021
  * Thesis: "Disengagement or Disillusionment? High Speed Internet and Political Participation"

Work experience
======
* 2025-Present: Senior Economist, The Centre for International Economics
  * Built development feasibility modelling capability using novel data and machine-learning techniques for clients including NSW Treasury, Queensland Productivity Commission, Infrastructure Victoria, NSW Cabinet Office and NSW Department of Planning.
  * Developed datasets and tools using retrieval augmented generation, local and cloud-based AI models, embedding models and text extraction at scale.
  * Drove team adoption of AI and agentic workflows, including agentic paper review and automation of substantial parts of the proposal process.
* 2026: Associate, Housing and Economic Security, Grattan Institute
  * Co-authored *Wasted Space: Axe Car-Parking Rules to Ease the Housing Crisis*.
  * Developed a machine-learning approach to produce Australian-first evidence on on-street parking supply and utilisation from aerial imagery.
  * Supported report release through live radio and newspaper interviews.
* 2022-2025: Senior Analyst, Transport and Urban Economics, Deloitte Access Economics
  * Contributed to Australia-wide small-area economic forecasting model D.ATLAS, drawing on datasets including satellite imagery and building structure data.
  * Undertook cost-benefit analysis using NSW Treasury guidelines for transport infrastructure investments and planning regulation changes.
  * Contributed to public-facing thought leadership, including housing and Western Sydney economic analysis.
  * Developed R data pipelines for major data sources, including the NSW DPHI DA API, Fair Trading rental bond data and Valuer General land values and sales data.
* 2019-2020: Intern, BIS Oxford Economics, now Oxford Economics Australia
